# Chapter 37: Self-Transformation Formula

**Guodian:** Bundle A, Slip A13 (~300 BCE)
**RSM Function:** Self-organization through non-imposition
**Book IV:** Self-Organization (Position 7)

---

## Chinese Text

道常無為而無不為
侯王若能守之
萬物將自化
化而欲作
吾將鎮之以無名之樸
夫亦將知止
知止可以不殆
譬道之在天下
猶川谷之與江海

## Pinyin

huà ér yù zuò
wú jiāng zhèn zhī yǐ wú míng zhī pǔ

zhī zhǐ kě yǐ bù dài

yóu chuān gǔ zhī yǔ jiāng hǎi

## Structural Translation

Pattern constantly non-imposes, yet nothing remains un-acted.
If nobles and rulers could guard this:
All things will self-transform.
Transforming, when desire arises to impose,
I will anchor it with the nameless uncarved block.
Thus they too will come to know stopping.
Knowing stopping, one can avoid peril.
Pattern's presence in the world:
Like streams and valleys with rivers and seas.

## Key Operations

### 無為 (wú wéi) - Non-Imposition
**Not:** Inaction, passivity
**But:** Acting without forcing against pattern

**Chapter 64 definition:** 輔萬物之自然而不敢為 (assist self-so-ness, not daring to impose)

### 自化 (zì huà) - Self-Transformation
**Radicals:** 自 (self, origin marker) + 化 (transformation)
**Operation:** Transformation originating from within
**Function:** System has internal transformation logic

**One of seven ancient 自X operations** (Guodian confirmed):

| Self-Operation | Chapter | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| 自化 | 37 | Self-transform |
| 自定 | 32 | Self-settle |
| 自然 | 64 | Self-so |
| 自均 | 32 | Self-equalize |
| 自富 | 57 | Self-prosper |
| 自正 | 57 | Self-correct |
| 自樸 | 57 | Self-simplify |

### 樸 (pǔ) - Uncarved Block
**Radicals:** 木 (wood) + 菐 (unspecified)
**Operation:** Pre-differentiated state
**Function:** Wood before specific forms imposed

Origin anchor. Material before naming creates boundaries.

### 鎮 (zhèn) - Anchor
**Radicals:** 金 (metal) + 真 (true)
**Operation:** Anchor to origin point
**Function:** Hold stable reference, prevent drift

**Not:** Suppress, control
**But:** Return to O point in O→G→P cycles

### 知止 (zhī zhǐ) - Know-Stopping
**Operation:** Recognize completion boundaries
**Function:** Prevent 殆 (peril) from pushing beyond functional range

**止** = foot planted, cessation of motion

## The Self-Transformation Formula

```
Pattern non-imposes (道常無為)
        ↓
Nothing remains un-acted (而無不為)
        ↓
IF rulers maintain this (侯王若能守之)
        ↓
THEN all things self-transform (萬物將自化)
        ↓
WHEN impulse to control arises (化而欲作)
        ↓
Return to origin state (鎮之以無名之樸)
        ↓
Learn stopping boundaries (知止)
        ↓
Avoid peril (可以不殆)
```

---

## The 若 Operator

**侯王若能守之** - IF nobles/rulers COULD guard this

**若 (ruò)** = appearance/conditional operator

**Not:** Simple "if"
**But:** "As if they could (but likely won't)"

Skepticism built into the operator. Creates conditional space between what pattern allows and what humans actually do.

---

## Stabilization Protocol

**化而欲作，吾將鎮之以無名之樸**

During transformation (化), when desire to impose arises (欲作):
→ Anchor (鎮) with nameless (無名) uncarved block (樸)
→ Return to pre-differentiated state before distinctions crystallized

**Mechanism:**
- 欲作 = impulse to control/force
- 樸 = origin state where specific forms haven't solidified
- Returning to 樸 = resetting to pre-imposition condition

---

## The 知止 Principle

**知止可以不殆**

**知止** appears throughout DDJ:

| Chapter | Text | Function |
|---------|------|----------|
| 32 | 知止所以不殆 | Know-stop therefore not perilous |
| 37 | 知止可以不殆 | Know-stop can avoid peril |
| 44 | 知止不殆 | Know-stop, not perilous |
| 46 | 知足之為足 | Know-sufficiency as sufficiency |

**止** = Recognition that every operation has completion boundary

**殆 (dài)** = Peril, danger from pushing beyond completion

**Scythe example:**
- Arc completes its sweep (π-operation done)
- Step forward (P becomes next O)
- Trying to sweep further = breaks blade (殆)

## The Hydraulic Metaphor

**譬道之在天下，猶川谷之與江海**

```
Small sources (川谷 - streams/valleys)
        ↓
Flow to (與 - in relation with)
        ↓
Large destinations (江海 - rivers/seas)
```

**Chapter 66 connection:** 江海為百谷王 (rivers/seas rule valleys by being below)

**Mechanism:**
- Low position receives all flows
- Valleys don't force, they position
- Seas rule through structure (below), not imposition

**Pattern's presence in world** = Like water systems that receive flows through position, not force

## The Complete Operation

**無為 (non-imposition)** creates space for **自化 (self-transformation)**

**Not:** Passivity, inaction
**But:** Building infrastructure that enables self-organization

**Examples:**
- Irrigation channels → fields self-water → people self-prosper
- Clear flow paths → distribution self-corrects
- Valley position → flows come naturally
- Terraces for water → self-leveling occurs

**Governance = 治 = 氵(water) + 台 (platform)**
Build structures supporting water's self-organization
