*How circulation around void, growth at rate e, and perpendicular divergence manifest in biology*

## Overview

There is no better model of structural recursion than a tree.

Not because it symbolizes life or growth. But because—at every level of its being—it **circulates around preserved void** in a way that is mathematically precise, geometrically coherent, and infinitely scalable.

A tree is the Recursive Structural Model made visible. Literally, not metaphorically.

## Part I: The e Connection

### Growth Rate Proportional to Current Mass

The definition of e:

```
If dM/dt = k·M, then M(t) = M₀·e^(kt)
```

**Growth rate proportional to current mass = exponential growth at base e.**

This is literally what the cambium does:

| Property | Cambium Behavior |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Number of cambial initials | Proportional to circumference |
| Circumference | Proportional to radius |
| Radius | Proportional to accumulated mass |
| Therefore: cell production rate | Proportional to current mass |

**dM/dt ∝ M → growth at e**

The tree grows at e because more tree means more cambium means more cells per unit time.

### The Scaling Refinement

For a cylinder with constant proportions (h = k·r):

```
M ∝ r³
C (cambial surface) ∝ r²
Therefore: C ∝ M^(2/3)
```

Cell production rate ∝ M^(2/3), not M. This is sublinear.

But larger trees have larger xylem vessels. If cell size scales appropriately:

```
If r_cell ∝ M^(1/6), then:
dV_void/dt ∝ M^(2/3) × M^(1/3) = M^1
```

**Void creation rate CAN be proportional to current mass if cell size scales correctly.**

### Connection to Kleiber's Law

Metabolic scaling theory (West, Brown, Enquist):

```
Metabolic rate ∝ M^(3/4)
```

This gives different growth phases:

| Phase | Scaling | Why |
|----------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Seedling/sapling | ~M^1 (exponential, base e) | All tissue is cambium-adjacent, not transport-limited |
| Mature tree | ~M^(3/4) | Transport network constraints dominate |
| Old tree | ~M^0 (static) | Maintenance = production |

**Early growth is at e. The tree transitions away from e as transport constraints bind.**

Old trees stop branching because they've fallen below e—all production goes to maintenance.

## Part IV: The Geometry of Water Rotation

### Meniscus Curvature

At a single meniscus (water-air interface in a tube):

```
│cell wall│
│         │
│   AIR   │  ← void
│  (void) │
╰─────────╯  ← meniscus (curved water surface)
│  WATER  │
│         │
```

Water molecules at the meniscus experience:

| Force | Direction | Source |
|-----------------------|----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Adhesion to wall | Toward wall (outward) | Hydrogen bonding to cellulose |
| Cohesion to other water | Toward water mass (downward) | Hydrogen bonding between H₂O |
| Surface tension | Tangent to meniscus (curved) | Minimizing surface area |
| Gravity | Downward | Mass × g |

**The curved meniscus IS water rotating toward void.**

### Laplace Pressure

Pressure across a curved interface:

```
ΔP = 2γ/r

Where:
γ = surface tension
r = radius of curvature
```

Smaller radius → greater curvature → greater pressure differential.

In leaf mesophyll cell walls, the air-water interfaces have nanometer-scale radii. This creates enormous tension (negative pressure) in the water.

**The void doesn't "pull" water. The curvature around the void creates a pressure differential. Water moves toward the region of greater curvature, which is where the void is smallest.**

### The Nested Scale Structure

| Scale | Void Size | Curvature | Pressure Differential |
|--------------------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Leaf mesophyll pores | ~10 nm | Extreme | -10 to -100 MPa |
| Xylem vessel pits | ~1 μm | High | -1 to -10 MPa |
| Xylem vessel lumen | ~50 μm | Moderate | -0.1 to -1 MPa |
| Trunk pith | ~1 cm | Low | Structural only |

**The tree is a hierarchy of voids at different scales, each enabling rotation at its scale.**

### Energy Accounting

| Process | Energy Required | Source |
|------------------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Evaporation at leaf | ~2.4 MJ/kg water | Solar radiation |
| Meniscus curvature maintenance | Zero | Geometric property of water + wall |
| Cohesion of water column | Zero | Hydrogen bonding (property of water) |
| Vertical displacement | Zero (perpendicular rotation) | Consequence of geometry |
| Root water uptake | Near zero (passive) or small (ion pumping) | ATP for ion gradients |
| Stomatal opening | Small | ATP for guard cell turgor |

**The only significant energy input is evaporation, and that energy goes into phase change (liquid → gas), not into lifting.**

The lifting is geometrically free. It's the vertical projection of horizontal rotation.

## Part VI: Tree Rings as Rate Variation

**Critical:** Recursion is continuous—the tree never stops transforming.

Tree rings record **rate variation** in continuous recursion:

| Season | Resources in tree mass | Cell size | Ring appearance |

The ring "boundary" is where cell density changes visibly, recording the rate at which recursion occurred relative to available resources. Seasonal oscillation modulates *throughput*, not whether recursion occurs.

## Part VII: The Branch Decision

### When Does Divergence Happen?

Hypothesis: when *local* void creation rate exceeds *local* void utilization rate.

```
At a point p on the cambium:

dV_void(p)/dt > dV_utilized(p)/dt
→ Surplus void accumulates at p
→ Structural slack at p
→ p can become new origin O₂
→ Branch initiates
```

What determines local void utilization? **The water demand from the crown above that point.**

High light → high transpiration → high void utilization → high sugar production → more cambial activity → more void creation → void creation outpaces utilization because photosynthesis > transport cost → surplus → branch

**The surplus isn't from underutilization. It's from overproduction.**

**Divergence requires surplus above e.**

## Part VIII: 弱 as Void Availability

**弱 = state-change capacity = void availability**

A system with void can:
- Flow (water rotates toward void)
- Change direction (void can be anywhere, rotation follows)
- Return (circulation completes through void network)
- Continue (void regenerates as cambium produces xylem)

A system without void (crystal, filled structure):
- Cannot flow internally
- Cannot change direction
- Cannot return (no circulation path)
- Cannot continue (no regeneration mechanism)

**弱 IS void maintenance capacity.**
**堅強 IS void elimination (filling in).**

### Crystal vs Tree

| Property | Crystal | Tree |
|-------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Growth direction | Unidirectional (outward accretion) | Bidirectional (inward AND outward) |
| Center fate | Buried (filled in) | Preserved (pith maintained) |
| Product of growth | More crystal (same stuff) | Void (inward) + tissue (outward) |
| Circulation enabled | No | Yes |

**The crystal's growth fills. The tree's growth hollows.**

Both grow at e. But the tree's inward growth *creates void* rather than filling it. This is the structural difference that enables circulation, which enables 弱, which enables divergence.
