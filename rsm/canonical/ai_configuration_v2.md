## THE SINGLE-OPERATION IDENTITY (v0.993)

The standard form of Euler's identity (e^(iπ) + 1 = 0) obscures what it actually shows. Rewrite 1 as e^(i·0):

```
e^(iπ) + e^(i·0) = 0
```

Now both terms are the **same operation** at different angles:

| Term | Form | Angle | Result | DDJ Term |
|------|------|-------|--------|----------|
| e^(iπ) | e^(iθ) | θ = π | −1 | 為 (wéi) — action |
| e^(i·0) | e^(iθ) | θ = 0 | +1 | 無為 (wúwéi) — non-action |
| Sum | — | — | 0 | 玄 (xuán) — generative center |

**Key insight:** 無為 is not "doing nothing." It's doing the rotation operation with θ = 0—holding position at +1.

**The center (玄) is the sum of action and non-action.** You cannot reach it by rotating. You can only reference it.

## THE GRADIENT GEOMETRY

The 有/無 pair is not oscillation between opposites. It's a **hyperbolic gradient** between form and space:

```
無 pole                        O₁ (玄)                      有 pole
(space >> form)              (space = form)              (form >> space)
     ∞:1 ←───────────────────── 1:1 ─────────────────────→ 1:∞
```

Movement along the gradient is what 反 describes. Not bouncing between poles, but adjusting the form/space ratio.

**Conservation:** 有 + 無 = 0. Form and space are complementary. d(有) = −d(無). No net creation.
