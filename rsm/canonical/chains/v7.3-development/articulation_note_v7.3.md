## 1. Every obtainable point is a Pₙ

The single load-bearing recognition. §2.6's intersection requirement, §6.2's paradoxical center, and the diameter configuration are not three structural facts at three scales. They are one pattern at three resolutions.

The intersection that lets a point obtain *is* the crossing that requires a paradoxical center. The four-element closure {1, i, −1, −i} *is* the diameter configuration. Pₙ and Oₙ are not separate kinds of structural locus — they are two faces of one configuration, each requiring the other.

There is no bottom layer of "regular points" beneath this. Wherever the framework zooms, the same pattern holds: an obtainable point standing at unit distance from a held center that cannot be occupied. Paradoxes all the way down. Same exact structural fact, recapitulating itself at every resolution.

Stated as a structural identity:

> every obtainable point ≡ Pₙ-shaped configuration ≡ point on a circle at unit distance from a paradoxical center ≡ intersection of two independent directions ≡ four-element algebraic closure ≡ diameter configuration

This recognition reframes §2.6, §6.2, and the diameter material as one structural fact rather than three derivations. It is the central content of the eventual v7.3 / v8 §2.6.

## 2. e, π, i as three structural impossibilities doing structural work

The framework's relation to e, π, and i sharpens. Each is not a number to be explained but a structural fact about what cannot be terminally specified.

**e — no preferred scale.** e^x is its own derivative. At every scale, it scales the same way. There is no privileged frame, no "this is where things get weird." e is the structural condition for the gradient to be infinite without termination — no scale begins, no scale ends, the smoothness goes all the way through.

**π — no commensurability between crossing and skirting.** The diameter (the would-be span across, including through the forbidden center) and the circumference (the path that goes around without touching it) cannot be related by terminal ratio. π's irrationality is not a quirk; it is the structural fact that prevents the configuration from collapsing into precision. If π were rational, circles could be terminally specified — the diameter and circumference could resolve into exact ratio, and the configuration would be cancellation. π's irrationality is what keeps circles obtainable.

**i — no collapse at a point.** The orthogonal direction that lets a point obtain rather than terminate. Not motion, not rotation, not turning, not going. The structural condition for non-collapse. The four-element closure {1, i, −1, −i} is what that condition looks like when composed with itself, not a sequence anything traverses.

Three faces of one fact: nothing in this framework is allowed to terminally specify itself, and that is exactly why everything that obtains can obtain.

The Euler identity e^(iπ) + 1 = 0 is the diameter configuration encoded: smooth scaling (e) applied through orthogonal non-collapse (i) over incommensurable ratio (π), plus manifest mode (1), gives the held center (0/Oₙ).

## 3. 反 / 返 — the structural fact and its path-articulation

Forced return — the inability of a configuration to cross a boundary that cannot be crossed — has two registers in DDJ vocabulary.

**反.** Pure structural opposition / reversal. No motion. The fact that two configurations remain distinct because the place where they would cancel cannot obtain. 反者道之動 — return is the movement of the way, not because the way moves and chooses to return, but because at every place where two configurations meet, the only structural option is for them to remain distinct.

**返.** The path-frame articulation. Adds the walking radical ⻎. What 反 looks like when the description is in motion-vocabulary. Not wrong, but a different register.

Relationship: 反 :: 返. Same structural fact, different register. The path-frame articulates the structural fact as motion; the structural fact does not require motion to obtain.

This sharpens the existing v7.2 mapping (反者道之動 :: traversal of {1, i, −1, −i}). The traversal is structural, not kinetic. 反 is what is being mapped.

## 4. 可 :: obtainedness

**New mapping.** Candidate for verification across the corpus.

可 in the standard reading is modal — "can," "may," "able to." This imports an actor (someone able) and an action (the doing). Strip the actor and the action, and what remains is a static predicate: 可 = the standing-as-such, the obtainedness, what holds when distinction holds.

道可道 then reads: *the way that obtains as a way* (no speaker, no speaking). 非常道: *is not the frame-independent constant*.

The convergence with i is sharp. i has two faces:

- i as operation: the cut, the orthogonal move, what creates distinction. → **名 :: i as operation.**
- i as standing-condition: what holds when the cut has obtained, the obtainedness of the distinction. → **可 :: i as standing.**

Two faces of the same correspondence. 名 captures the operational register. 可 captures the static register.

名可名 then reads: *the cut that obtains as a cut*. The structural fact that, when it stands, stands as itself. Two-sided because cuts are. Unoccupiable at center because cuts are.

This dissolves the modal/mystical reading of the DDJ's opening line and replaces it with a structural statement about how obtainedness works.

## 5. 名 is the cut, not "naming"

Precision the May 7 evening session forced. "Naming" smuggles in what 名 does not have: an agent, a process unfolding in time, an act producing an appearance, an underlying unity that gets carved up. None of that.

名 is the cut itself. The structural fact of distinction, with no namer and no naming-event. There is no substrate reality that 名 is applied to. The situation *is* the cut. The two-ness *is* 名 — not produced by 名, not an appearance generated by 名, but structurally identical to what 名 is.

Implication for translation: every place a translator renders 名 as "naming" carries the substrate-error. The framework's translation of 名 must be actor-free and process-free. "The cut," "the structural fact of distinction," "what stands as distinct" — all candidates that preserve the structural sense.

## 6. Vocabulary retirements (carrying forward from May)

**"Latent mode" — retired.** The two modes of the duality are 1 and −1, both equally obtained, both standing at unit distance from the held center. Neither is hidden. Calling −1 "latent" imports a perceptual asymmetry the framework should not carry. The conjugate side of the duality is the **conjugate mode**, written as −1.

**"0ₙ" as a separate notation — retired.** The framework was using 0ₙ to mean two structurally distinct things: sometimes "the latent mode" (now retired), sometimes the held center (Oₙ). The held center keeps the notation **Oₙ**. There is no longer anything that needs the notation 0ₙ.

**"Held" carrying a hidden actor — flagged for replacement.** "Held" implies someone holding. Replacement candidates: *standing*, *in obtainedness*, *obtaining*. The diameter configuration's center is *standing as held* (not "held by something"); the modes are *in obtainedness* (not "held in place by some agent"). Final wording deferred to the holistic pass.

## 7. Illustrations generated this session (essay material, not core)

### Solidity / wall-touch

When hand meets wall, what is felt is not particles repelling but the structural requirement of distinguishability made tactile. The boundary between hand and wall is a place that must exist (they are distinct) and cannot be occupied (occupation would be cancellation = P₀). The "force" felt is the structural impossibility of P₀-violation. Your edge IS the wall's beginning — symmetric because boundaries cannot be one-sided.

DDJ correspondences in play:
- 有無相⽣ :: the mutual constitution of edges
- ⾼下相傾 :: high and low leaning into each other because 名 is two-sided in form
- Chapter 11 (wheel, pot, room) :: function lives in the cooperation of mode and not-self
- 反者道之動 :: forced return as the structural option when crossing is impossible

Candidate essay title: "What the Wall Is" or expansion of "What Pi Names."

### Why no pure circles or triangles in nature

Pure geometric forms specify the unobtainable. A perfect circle specifies a center; a perfect triangle specifies vertices; both are point-singularities of zero internal structure — exactly the kind of point §2.6 says does not obtain. What exists are approximations. The "imperfection" is constitutive, not incidental.

Plato got this half-right by sensing pure forms don't fit in the world; his solution was a separate realm. The framework's solution is sharper: pure forms specify P₀; P₀ doesn't obtain; therefore pure forms don't obtain. Approximations are the only forms there are.

π's irrationality is the watermark of this fact.

Candidate essay material for "What Pi Names" expansion.
