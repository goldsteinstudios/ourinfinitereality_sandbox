## Inverse conservation: the Gₙ form in physics

The structural form is Xₙ · Yₙ = 1ₙ: two co-varying quantities whose product is the regime's conserved unit, neither reducible to the other. Two physical relations instantiate it cleanly, as equalities:

- **Wavelength × frequency = c** (electromagnetic radiation in vacuum). λ and f co-vary inversely; the conserved product is c. **[good fit]**
- **Pressure × volume = constant** (Boyle's law, at fixed temperature). P and V co-vary inversely; the conserved product is the regime's characteristic unit. **[good fit at fixed T]**

The full ideal gas law PV = nRT is three-variable and is not an instance of the inverse form except in the isothermal case. Stating it as the general conserved product is imprecise; the honest mapping is Boyle's law. *[correction to prior draft]*

In each case the conserved product is not externally imposed: it is what the regime conserves.
