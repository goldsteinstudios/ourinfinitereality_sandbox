# The Recursive Structural Model and Relativity: Local Orthogonality, Global Curvature

## Abstract  
Relativity and the Recursive Structural Model (RSM) both describe reality as **locally simple yet globally curved**. Einstein framed this as “spacetime is locally flat, globally curved.” The RSM frames it as “recursion is locally orthogonal, globally curved.” This paper develops a direct mapping between the two frameworks, demonstrating that relativity’s physics and the RSM’s structural logic are convergent descriptions of the same necessity: paradox preservation across scales.  

---

## 1. Introduction  
- Einstein’s General Relativity revolutionized physics by replacing force with geometry: matter and energy tell spacetime how to curve.  
- The Recursive Structural Model (RSM) begins from logical impossibility (void forbidden) and derives structural necessities: gradients, paradox, recursion, curvature.  
- Both approaches converge on the principle: **local independence requires global curvature**.  

---

## 2. Local vs. Global: Two Languages  

### Relativity  
- *Local flatness*: any small region can be treated as Euclidean.  
- *Global curvature*: stitch regions together → manifold curvature appears.  

### RSM  
- *Local orthogonality*: each recursion step is an independent 90° deflection (*i*).  
- *Global curvature*: accumulated orthogonal turns yield circulation (π) and shell closure (sphere).
