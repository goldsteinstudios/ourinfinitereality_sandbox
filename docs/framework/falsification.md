# RSM Falsification Criteria
## What Would Disprove the Recursive Structural Model

---

## Importance of Falsifiability

A framework that cannot be wrong cannot be right. RSM commits to specific predictions that can be empirically tested and theoretically challenged.

---

## Empirical Falsification Conditions

### 1. Discovery of Scale Termination
**If found**: A fundamental smallest unit that cannot be further divided.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: RSM requires infinite divisibility. Any absolute scale floor would contradict the core axiom that reality is infinitely divisible.

**Test domain**: Quantum gravity experiments, Planck-scale physics

**Current status**: Not observed. Planck scale remains theoretical.

---

### 2. Violation of E ∝ 1/r
**If found**: Precision access that doesn't cost energy, or energy-precision relationship follows different scaling.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: CAVP requires that accessing finer precision always costs more energy, with inverse relationship.

**Test domains**:
- Resolution vs. energy in microscopes/telescopes
- Metabolic cost vs. sensory refinement in biology
- Precision vs. processing energy in computation

**Current status**: Holds across all tested domains.

---

### 3. Rational Frequency Superiority
**If found**: Systems with rational frequency ratios consistently outperform irrational ones for stability.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: RSM predicts irrational constants (π, e, φ) are structurally necessary for preventing periodic collapse.

**Test domains**:
- Oscillatory circuit coherence
- Neural oscillation stability
- Crystal structure optimization

**Current status**: Irrational ratios (especially golden ratio) show superior stability in quasicrystals and biological systems.

---

### 4. Strictly Localized Information
**If found**: Information that exists without any field or relational structure — pure point-information with no gradient context.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: RSM treats information as distributed across proportional relationships, never localized in isolated states.

**Test domains**:
- Quantum information theory
- Black hole information paradox resolution
- Neural encoding mechanisms

**Current status**: All known information requires relational structure.

---

### 5. Conservation Violation Under Wu Wei
**If found**: A system maintaining ∂Pₙ/∂t = 0 (paradox preservation) while violating energy, momentum, or angular momentum conservation.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: RSM equates wu wei (∂Pₙ/∂t = 0) with simultaneous satisfaction of all conservation laws.

**Test domain**: Any physical system

**Current status**: Conservation laws remain unviolated.

---

## Theoretical Falsification Conditions

### 1. Unresolvable Internal Contradiction
**If found**: Two RSM principles that cannot both be true within the framework.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: Internal consistency is required for any valid model.

**Current status**: No contradictions identified.

---

### 2. Superior Explanatory Framework
**If found**: A framework that explains all RSM phenomena plus additional phenomena RSM cannot explain, with equal or greater parsimony.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: A more powerful framework renders the weaker one obsolete.

**Current status**: No such framework proposed. RSM integrates with existing frameworks (Wheeler, Bohm, Rovelli, etc.) rather than competing.

---

### 3. Y₁ · X₁ ≠ k in Stable Systems
**If found**: A demonstrated stable recursive system where the proportional constraint is violated.

**Why it falsifies RSM**: The hyperbolic gradient field is the minimal structure surviving infinite divisibility.

**Test domain**: Any stable system at any scale

**Current status**: Proportional relationships observed universally.

## What Would NOT Falsify RSM

1. **Finding new phenomena**: RSM is a framework, not a complete theory. New phenomena should fit within, not contradict, the structure.

2. **Better precision on constants**: Measuring π or e to more digits doesn't change that they're irrational.

3. **Different vocabularies**: Different fields using different terms for the same structure is expected (G3: "Different languages, one grammar").

4. **Apparent contradictions between scales**: CAVP explains why local discreteness and global continuity are compatible.

---

## The Commitment

RSM makes concrete, testable predictions. If these predictions fail:
- The energy-precision relationship is wrong
- The irrational constant necessity is wrong
- The proportional constraint is wrong
- The wu wei/conservation equivalence is wrong

Any of these would require fundamental revision or abandonment of RSM.
