## Guodian Evidence

**潰 (kuì) instead of 逝 (shì)**

Guodian manuscripts (~300 BCE) use 潰 (overflow, burst dam) instead of received text's 逝 (depart).

**Significance:**
- More hydraulic/geometric
- Less volitional (water doesn't choose to overflow)
- Emphasizes structural necessity over intentional action

**This supports:** Reading the text as geometric documentation, not philosophical choice
