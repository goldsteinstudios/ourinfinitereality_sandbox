### 5.2 The Hamon Principle

When forging a blade, the smith faces a fundamental problem: the edge must be hard (剛) to cut, but the spine must be flexible (柔) to absorb impact without shattering.

The smiths of ancient China and Japan solved this through differential hardening:

1. Apply clay—thick on the spine, thin on the edge
2. Heat the blade uniformly
3. Quench in water

**Result:**
- **Exposed edge**: Rapid cooling → hard crystalline structure (剛)
- **Clay-covered spine**: Slow cooling → flexible structure (柔)
- **The boundary**: The hamon—visible gradient where properties meet

**The blade is Chapter 76 written in steel.**

A blade that is entirely 剛 shatters on first impact.
A blade that is entirely 柔 bends and won't cut.
The functional blade has *both properties in correct distribution*.

This is not moral advice. It is material science.
