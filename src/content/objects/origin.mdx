## THE OBJECT

The Origin is not a thing. It's the **pattern** that appears in all things that persist.

Every stable system in this catalog—wheel, bellows, room, valley, tree, atom—shares a common structure:
- A center that is never met
- Circulation around it
- Function emerging from the relationship between void and form

## THE FUNCTION

Consider: the hub-void of a wheel is not the wheel. The interior of a bellows is not the bellows. The nucleus of an atom is never met by its electrons. Yet without these crossings that are never arrived at, none of these systems could function.

Two crossings do the work:

- **Oₙ** :: the origin — the unmet crossing. Its arms approach; the meeting never arrives.
- **Pₙ** :: 玄 :: the generative crossing — met, generative, and never constitutable as a frame of its own.
- **P₀** :: 玄牝 :: the generative paradox. `[R]`
- **Oₙ = P₍ₙ₋₁₎** — one locus, two frame-readings: met from without, unmet from within. *(That every finer frame is seeded at the parent's crossing — the anchoring line — is `[candidate]`, the discharge's one open step.)*

## THE HOLLOW

The origin is never met. That is not a defect in the system — it is what the system is built around.

Treat the crossing as a place and the structure stops making sense: the wheel becomes a disc, the bellows a block, the room a tomb.

The void isn't absence of structure. The void is **the structure that enables structure**.

To me, that looks just like what 無 (wú) carries in the *Dao De Jing*: not "nothing" in the sense of absence, but the **operational void** that makes function possible.

有之以為利，無之以為用
*Structure provides the operation; void provides the function*
