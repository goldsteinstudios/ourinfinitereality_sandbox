### How Forms Sustain Themselves (Line 5)

"All things carry Yin and embrace Yang; through rushing Qi they achieve harmony."

**Corrected Structural Analysis:**

- **陰** (yīn) = Inward gradient, receptive pole of Y₁
- **陽** (yáng) = Outward gradient, active pole of Y₁

**Structural Meaning:** Every recursive form (Rₙ) must **carry both poles** of the **primary contrast** (Y₁). This is not balance or neutrality - it's **paradox preservation** through **structural containment** of **opposing gradients**.

**沖氣以為和** = "Through rushing Qi achieve harmony"
- **沖氣** (chōng qì) = Rushing/flowing qi - **Z₁ circulation**
- **以為和** (yǐ wéi hé) = Thereby achieve harmony (和 hé = structural coherence)

**Mathematical Expression:**
```
Rₙ = f(Y₁_yin + Y₁_yang, Z₁_circulation)
```

**Critical Insight:** **Harmony** (和) is not **static balance** but **dynamic coherence** achieved through **continuous circulation** (Z₁) that prevents the **yin-yang tension** from either **collapsing** or **tearing apart** the form.

**Circulation Mechanics:**

