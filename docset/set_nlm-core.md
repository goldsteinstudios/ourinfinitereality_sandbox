### 1.1. The V₀ Prohibition: The Dissolution of "Nothing"

The model begins by defining absolute void (V₀) as the complete absence of distinction, contrast, content, and structure. It is a concept of total non-existence that cannot be a state, location, origin, or destination. From this definition, the model's first theorem follows directly.

This is not a metaphysical claim about what may or may not exist in some unknowable sense; it is a formal limitation on what can be coherently described. The question "Why is there something rather than nothing?" presupposes that "nothing" names a coherent alternative. If V₀ is unspecifiable, the question is ill-posed, much like asking, "What is north of the North Pole?" This prohibition is the logical bedrock from which all other structural necessities emerge. If absolute void is prohibited from description, then contrast is necessary for coherence.

### 1.2. The Generative Center (O₁): A Consequence of Contrast

If V₀ is prohibited, contrast is necessary for any coherent description. The simplest model of contrast is a continuous gradient between two opposed poles, such as Form (有) and Space (無). The infinite divisibility of the gradient between Form and Space, combined with the unoccupiability of the O₁ center, creates a profound "measurement crisis." **Contrast. Rotation. Closure.**
