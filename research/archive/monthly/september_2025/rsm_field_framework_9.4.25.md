## Abstract

The Recursive Structural Model (RSM) explains how reality maintains coherent structure under infinite divisibility. Everyday examples illustrate this principle: a spinning top remains upright only through motion; a planet sustains orbit through circulation; a microscope requires more energy to reveal finer detail. Each demonstrates the trade-off between circulation and energy that stabilizes structure.

RSM formalizes this as a proportional gradient field $G_1$, defined by contrast ($Y_1$) and dimensional axis ($X_1$) such that $X_1Y_1 = 1$. Within this field, a balance point ($P_1$) and an origin frame ($O_1$) arise as necessary features. Absolute void ($P_0$) without not-void is structurally impossible; distinction emerges immediately, without sequence or agency. Information is reframed as relationships distributed across fields—always accurate, accessed with variable precision.  

RSM integrates with existing theories while remaining falsifiable through empirical tests. It offers an agentless substrate that generates the diversity of forms we observe.  

**Preview of Oneness.** Later sections (Appendix A) describe oneness as orbit: at each scale, native circulation defines unity for that level. Introducing this here links technical field theory to its philosophical resonance—balance experienced as wholeness, whether in a breath, an orbit, or universal expansion.

## 2. Minimal Structure Derivation

### 2.1 From Paradox to Gradient
Absolute void ($P_0$) collapses immediately into distinction: void implies not-void. No sequence, no cause—only co-emergence. The minimal non-collapsing structure is a proportional relation between dimension ($X_1$) and contrast ($Y_1$).

### 2.2 Gradient Field $G_1$
Plain explanation: imagine contrast and dimension as two sliders—when one increases, the other decreases so their product stays fixed.  

Formally:  
$$
G_1 = \{(X_1, Y_1) \in \mathbb R_+^2 \mid X_1Y_1 = 1\}.
$$

### 2.3 Balance and Origin
On $G_1$, the fixed locus $X_1 = Y_1 = 1$ defines the balance point ($P_1$). Yet $P_1$ cannot exist alone—it co-emerges with an orienting reference, the origin frame ($O_1$).  

Minimality check: linear, circular, or discrete forms collapse under infinite refinement. The hyperbolic proportional field uniquely avoids collapse.

## 3. Stability and Circulation

### 3.1 Necessity of Circulation
A system at $P_1$ cannot remain static—it must circulate to preserve paradox. The minimal stability operation is unimodular scaling:  

$$
(X_1, Y_1) \mapsto (\lambda X_1, Y_1/\lambda), \quad \lambda > 0,
$$

which preserves $X_1Y_1 = 1$. In logarithmic coordinates $(u,v) = (\ln X_1, \ln Y_1)$ with $u+v=0$, this is a hyperbolic rotation about the balance condition.

### 3.2 Dimensionality Requirement
In 2D, circulation collapses into centers or saddles (Poincaré–Bendixson). Persistent circulation requires at least 3 orthogonal axes. Non-integrability in 3D ensures sustainable circulation. Odd dimensions generalize stability; even dimensions allow cancellations incompatible with paradox preservation.

## 5. Constant Accuracy, Variable Precision (CAVP)

* **Accuracy:** Invariant mathematical relations (e.g., $X_1Y_1 = 1$). Always true, regardless of resolution.  
* **Precision:** Degree of refinement in accessing accuracy. Always costly in energy.  

Examples:  
- Digits of π (mathematics)  
- Resolution of telescopes/microscopes (instrumentation)  
- Neural discrimination vs metabolic cost (biology)  
- Attention sharpness vs effort (consciousness)  

This accuracy–precision split anchors RSM’s empirical predictions.
